The Beginning of the Revolt

Date: March 29, 1857, Barrackpore

Mangal Pandey, a sepoy of the 34th Bengal Native Infantry, became one of the first known rebels against the British. He attacked British officers for enforcing the use of the Enfield rifle cartridges, which were rumored to be greased with cow and pig fat. His actions inspired unrest among sepoys across India.

Events:

  • Pandey attacked Lieutenant Baugh and called for revolt.
  • He was overpowered by British soldiers and arrested.
  • On April 8, 1857, Mangal Pandey was hanged.

Meerut & The Spread of the Revolt

Date: May 10, 1857, Meerut

The revolt spread to Meerut where Indian soldiers (sepoys) killed their British officers and marched to Delhi. This marked the beginning of widespread rebellion across northern and central India.

Events:

  • 85 sepoys refused to use the new cartridges and were imprisoned.
  • Other sepoys revolted, freed the imprisoned soldiers, and killed British officers.
  • The rebels marched to Delhi, where they were joined by more soldiers and civilians.

Delhi & The Siege

Date: May 11, 1857, Delhi

The rebels captured Delhi and proclaimed Bahadur Shah II, the Mughal emperor, as their leader. The British forces laid siege to the city, leading to a prolonged and bloody battle.

Events:

  • Rebels took control of Delhi and declared Bahadur Shah II as the emperor of India.
  • British forces, led by General John Nicholson, laid siege to Delhi.
  • After months of fighting, the British recaptured Delhi in September 1857.

Kanpur & The Massacre

Date: June 5, 1857, Kanpur

One of the most tragic events of the revolt occurred in Kanpur, where British women and children were massacred by the rebels. This event intensified the British resolve to crush the rebellion.

Events:

  • Rebels, led by Nana Sahib, besieged the British entrenchment at Kanpur.
  • After a prolonged siege, the British surrendered, but were massacred while evacuating.
  • The incident led to brutal reprisals by the British forces.

Lucknow & The Siege of the British Residency

Date: June - November 1857, Lucknow

Why Lucknow?

Begum Hazrat Mahal, the widow of the deposed Nawab of Awadh, led the revolt. She wanted to restore Awadh’s independence.

Events:

  • Rebels laid siege to the British Residency, where British officials and soldiers were trapped for months.
  • Supplies ran low, and many British died from starvation, disease, and attacks.
  • November 1857: British forces, under Colin Campbell, launched a counterattack.
  • March 1858: The British completely recaptured Lucknow.

Jhansi & The Rani's Resistance

Date: June 1857 - April 1858, Jhansi

Rani Lakshmibai of Jhansi became one of the most iconic leaders of the revolt. She fiercely defended her kingdom against the British forces.

Events:

  • Rani Lakshmibai formed an army and led the defense of Jhansi.
  • British forces, led by Sir Hugh Rose, besieged Jhansi in March 1858.
  • After intense fighting, the British captured Jhansi in April 1858.
  • Rani Lakshmibai escaped and continued to fight until her death in June 1858.
